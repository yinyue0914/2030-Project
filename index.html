Computer Color Models

Computer color models define standards used to represent, store, display, and manipulate colors in computers.
Two primary color models

RGB

RGB is an additive model, where a color is generated by combining the light intensity of the three colors red, green, and blue.
RGB is used in digital screens (monitors, TVs, cameras).

CMYK

CMYK is a subtractive model, where a color is generated by absorbing the light intensity of the three colors cyan, magenta, and yellow.
CMYK is used in printing.
